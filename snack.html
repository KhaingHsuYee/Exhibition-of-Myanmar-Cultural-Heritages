Myanmar Delicious Snacks

Myanmar Street Snacks

Myanmar has got varieties of snacks depending on the region or ethnic groups.Most famous mouth-watering snacks are listed here and you may easily find them in Traditional Restaurants or on the streets.

1.Tea Leaf Salad

Tea Leaf Salad(La Phat Thoke) is a traditional Myanmar food which is made from tea leaves and mixed with other ingredients.The texture of the salad is unique,the taste is a combination of soft,bitter,sour and also spicy.You can try it in restaurants as well as streetside stalls.Once you try it,you will understand why Burmese people love it.

2.Burmese Tempura

Burmese Tempura (Akyaw Sone) is essential for Burmese's people breadfast and favourite of all and love to enjoy with steamed rice.Sometimes burmese people enjoy Akyaw Sone just as a snack.Burmese Tempura includes a variety of things which are deep fried like onion,potato,peas,bananas,shrimp and so on.And also,Burmese Tempura cannot be perfect without its sauce.Enjoy Burmese Tempura street food with a cup of plain tea just like a local when you are in Myanmar.

3.Si Htamin

In the early morning,Myanmar people usually have warm or hot snacks as their breakfast.Si Htamin(Yellow Sticky Rice)is one of the popular local breadfast items.Enjoy this simple breadfast with a cup of coffee or burmese tea.

4.Kaut-Nyin-Paung

The best choice for people who likes steamed sticky rice! In Myanmar,Kaut-Nyin-Paung is mostly served for the breakfast or sometime at the tea time in the afternoon.It is one of the dishes always can be easily bought in markets and tea shops.

5.Htamane

Htamane is a traditional Burmese specialty consisting of glutinous rice,coconut shavings,peanuts,ginger,sesame seeds, water,and groundnut oil.The preparation of this savory snack is a communal activity that's associated with the annual Htamane Festival held around the Full Moon Day of the lunar month of Tabodwe(February).During this festivity,htamane is prepared in large quantities to satisfy a large number of people.Teams of htamane-makers participate in competitions that are traditionally held throughout the whole country as part of the rice festival.

6.Khaw Poat

Khaw Poat is a kind of appetizing snack of the Shan national race.That is a very traditional snack in Shan State made with black and white sticky rice,pounded with sesame seeds,salts and brown slab sugar.It is more tasty un-fried or unroasted and if recently pounded.

7.Mote Lin Ma Yar

This is a quite popular Myanmar Street Food and so decilious.That snack name is interesting.Lin ma yar means a couple: a husband and wife.Myanmar street hawkers do not use recipe but know how thick or thin they need their mix and the two segments are put together facing each other making them a loving couple(mont-linmayar).

8.Shwe-yin-Aye

Shwe-yin-Aye is a traditional Burmese dessert commonly associated with the Thingyan season.The dessert consists of sweetend sticky rice,sago pearls,pandan jelly noddles,and cubes of kyaukkyaw,coconut jelly,and a slice of white bread steeped in a concoction of sweetened coconut milk,served cold.

9.Mont Lone Yay Paw

One of the famous traditional Burmese sweet is "Mont-Lone-Yay-Paw" literally means "rice ball on the water".In Myanmar, it is made by boiling up balls of rice dough which pop up on top of the bubbling water when they are ready to eat.You can make them on your own or with friends and neighbours helping you in cheerful community spirits characteristic of Thingyan and the Myanmar New Year.

9.Laphet Tea

Of all the fruit,mango is the best;of all the meat,pork;and of all the leaves,laphet.Cultivating tea leaves has part of Myanmar's history for a long time and it has become a part of Myanmar's culture where people drink laphet tea(which we call Yay-Nway-Chan).Whenever the visitors reached the home,Myanmar people would entertain with laphet tea. This is our lovely habit!
