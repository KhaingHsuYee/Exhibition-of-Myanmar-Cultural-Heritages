Gold , Silverware & Embroidery

A Brief Note about Gold&Silverware .....

Besides, the things that are made with wood,metal and any other , you can see suprisingly the artifacts made with 'gold and silver'. Dating back in History, Myanmar has been the richest country in Asia ,and regarde as the 'Golden land' due to its plentiful golden temples, adornment and artefacts made with gold and silver. Since the past 1200 years ago(in the era of King Anawrahta), Myanmar's traditional arts and crafts artistic creation of gold and silverwares which is called the art work of genre('Badain') were belonged to. All the Myanmar traditional gold and silversmiths are manufactured by hand and that makes Myanmar's work of art impressed and unique. In long time ago, Monarchs customarily used silver and gold bowls to reward ministers and the court attendants for their loyalty to the royal family. Silver ittems were also a status symbol because only the ministers, the generals and the rich citizens used silver for everyday items such as flower bowls, vases,knife sheaths and spittoons.Could not afford the monks and Buddha statues and tools used for the poorest, silverware and silver altar during religious purposes, but can't be used to luxury. In modern times, these have also been deployed to provide souvenirs to visitors to Myanmar.

'Silversmith is the art of work relating to making drinking bowl, receptacle bowl, prize-cup, shield and belt'

'Goldsmith is the art of making things like ear-plug,ear-drops, ear-ring, with a screw-on back piece, finger-ring bracelet, Pendent and necklace'.

P.s:Goldsmith & Silversmith Workshp in Ywa-ma village which is located 12km away from Nyaung Shwe is well known for its gold and silver ware. Here you can see the gold and silver are made all by hands of local craftsmen. The design is similar to the design of 80s, but it's so much nicer and unique than other place like Bali.

A Brief Note about Embroidery......

There is one more classical souvenirs that you should buy ,which is an amazing 'Myanmar traditional Tapestries'. These are gold embroidered items-known in Myanmar as'Shwe Chi Htoe' One of these art forms, gold embroidery, was traditionally used to adorn the clothing of the royal family and others of a wealthy disposition. Today, Gold Embroideries are made to adorn homes in Local and abroad.The traditional gold embroidered designs can be seen in paintings, placing them on the wall in living rooms, hotels and restaurants,locally. Also,these are popular among the foreigners. Traditional tapestries are made by placing soft cotton in parts of the base to create a raiseed appearance and define the shapes, after which parts of the surface are painted and others are decorated with sequins and other objects. For the most part, they are not gold, although the string used includes gold threads- the name instead refers more to their colorful appearance.

The embroidery techniques are used to decorate items such as Myanmar costumes,purses and crowns used in no-vitation ceremonies. Costumes in gold embroidery are found in mostly Myanmar dramatic art in which dramatic actors and actresses wear embroidery costumes.Moreover,upgraded embroidety techniques can be seen in Myanmar traditional wedding dresses.

P.s:Mandalay has three large producers, and quite a number of small workshops set up in houses.They sell mostly to souvenir shop owners in sites such as Mingun, Inle lake and Bagan, where they are popular with visitors from Germany,France and Thailand. Prices vary significantly depending on the design,size and quality of the tapestry.
