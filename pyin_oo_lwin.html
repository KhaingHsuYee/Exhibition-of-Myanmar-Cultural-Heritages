The Best Things to do in Pyin Oo Lwin

Pyin Oo Lwin will take you back to 19th century Britain with its colonial red-coloured buildings and horse-driven carriages.owever,the buildings are not like the colonial buildings of Yangon and Mandalay.They are cute brick structures that will surely win your heart.Pyin Oo Lwin is home to the historic National Kandawgyi Botanical Gardens,the country's largest Botanical site established in 1915.This garden contains 514 species of indigenous trees and 74 foreign species,as well as 300 types of orchids and 25 different roses.The garden have been modelled after the famous Kew Gardens of London.The best way to enjoy Pyin Oo Lwin is to simply take a walk and immerse yourself in the hilly atmosphere.

1.Mahar Antt Htoo Kan Thar Pagoda

The building of this Pagoda has a history of its own. The reason was that three marble Buddha Images sculpted in Mandalay were being transported to their planned destination in China. On this journey, one of the Buddha statues fell down from the truck and could not be reloaded due to its weight. After many attempts, this Image was left behind and the other two were taken on their way. A local Buddhist monk decided he would try faith. He sat for 7 days on this image and preached to the locals and recited teachings of Buddha. After 7 days the image was apparently easily lifted and placed in its current location and the local people built a pagoda as an offering to Buddha.

2.Behold the Kan Daw Gyi Botanical Garden

The National Kandawgyi Gardens (formerly National Botanical Gardens) is located in the Alpine town about 1.5 km south of Pyin Oo Lwin (formerly Maymyo), Myanmar. It was first founded by Alex Roger, a forest research officer, with the aid of Lady Cuffe, the botanist in 1915. It was modelled after the Kew Gardens of England. The original area of the Botanical Garden was 170 acres of land and 70 acres of water spread totaling 240 acres. The government declared as a state-regonized garden in 1917. In 1924, the site was declared as a Goverment Forest Reserve. The garden has many collection of flora, including foreign species, orchids of various species in orchid garden, dahlia gardens, rose gardens, many exotic fruits and shady trees. In 2000, the garden was upgraded to be a recreation garden and renamed it “Kandawgyi National Gardens”.

3.Kandawkalay lake

This beautiful lake is only a 20-30 minute walk from the centre of Pyin Oo Lwin.It is a wonderful place to visit in the evening at sunset time and could be done after a trip to the Kandawgyi Botanical Gardens.

4.National Landmarks Garden

Just before the Kandawgyi National Botanical Gardens is another must-see attractions,the National Landmarks Garden.It consists of scale models of well-known Landmarks from each State in Myanmar spread over 50acres of parkland.This extensive hilly park is dotted with representations of famous landmarks from around Myanmar. It's pretty tacky, but if you haven't got time to tour the entire country.

5.Horse Carriage

Wander around Pyin Oo Lwin town centre for a little while and you’ll notice a rather bizarre sight: horses hitched up to old-fashioned carriages painted in vivid pinks, greens, purples and reds. Adding to the bizarre time-travel sense of the town, this is an experience not to be missed.Hop on and enjoy a ride.This is the best way to see the town.And it's fun too.

6.Purcell Tower

Situated in the heart of the town of Pyin Oo Lwin,Purcell Tower is one of the most peculiar tourist places in Myanmar.The clock was created in celebration of the Silver Jubilee of the rule of King George V of Britain.Though there are many stories behind the making of the tower,it still rings the tune of Big Ben and plays 16 notes before the hour,making the visit even more delightful.

7.Colonial architecture

There are many British colonial houses and Christian churches in Pyin Oo Lwin.Most of the colonial houses are situated at the south end of the circular road.Wooden summer cottages and brick-and-timber houses characterise the architecture in Pyin Oo Lwin,which is quite unlike the grand colonial edifices found in Yangon or Mandalay.It's almost like stepping into 19th century England.

8.Governor House

The original Governor House at Pyin Oo Lwin (May Myo) was destroyed during World War ll destruction. The New Governor House was reconstructed by Htoo Com.Ltd.The new house is built on the foundation of the old house. The ambition of reconstruction is to get the original form and the real atmosphere of the original building for everyone who look this house.If you do one day trip to Pyinoolwin from Mandalay , you definitely have to go to this place.But you need to pay entrance fee 2 USD per person if you like to go inside the building and it is still worth to visit.

9.December Cafe and Milk Bar

Situated on the way of May Myo-Lashio Road as a garden or restaurants.Relaxing with flowers and Strawberry fields as one stopping place.Having lunch or snacks with fresh milk be appeased.Their favorite menu is fresh milk from dairy farm at behind of the garden. Also get experience how they farm for dairy cattle.

10.Bamboo Cafe & Restaurant

The Bamboo Cafe and Restaurant located in the beautiful National Kandawgyi Gardens,a perfect place to enjoy the view while dining.All the menus are available at a reasonable price and the staffs are well trained to serve you good services.The decoration is simple but compact and attractive.

11.Peik Chin Myaung Cave

Peik Chin Myaung Cave is located on the Lashio road near Wetwun village,12 miles east of Pyin Oo Lwin town.It is a limestone cave estimated between 230 million and 310 million years old.The cave is called Peik Chin Myaung as plenty of Peik Chin plants used to grow there.The long cave is filled with so many Buddha images and pagodas in various sizes and position at every corners and niches.

12.Croft Organic Farm

Croft Organic Farm is one of the famous places in Pyin Oo Lwin.Both local customers and tourists don't miss to visit there when they are in Pyin Oo Lwin.Camp name is "Le Camp De Vacances".Near National Kandawgyi Garden,Pyin Oo Lwin,May Myo.

13.Pwal Kaut Waterfall

The Pwal Kaut waterfalls is a very pleasant picinic spot in Pyin Oo Lwin where many Myanmar families come for a picinic.Pwal Kaut or B.E waterfalls is also known as Hampshire Falls in British times.It is located on the way to Laswahio and not too far from Pyin Oo Lwin.Pwal Kaut stunned by it's natural beauty and breathtaking scenery.

14.December Farm Waterfall

This waterfall was discovered recently.It is located on a famous farm named in December.You wll walk along the lovely road with Greenland.The water was drizzling like a sprinkler.Favorite place for young people.

15.Dee Doke Waterfall

Amazing waterfall with a blue color.It is located in near Own Chaw village.Now,it is easier to get there.It is more like a blue pool than waterfall.The waterfall has 10 layers and you can swim every single of the pool.And,the perfect place to take a dip.

16.Dattawgyaik Waterfall(Annisakan Waterfall)

At an impressive 120m tall,this Waterfall is genuinely extremely impressive and a little terrifying if you get up close.The spray from the Waterfalls dampens everything in the area.The ominous plunge pool is not safe for swimming.A visit to Dattawgyaik Waterfall is a must do if you're in Pyin Oo Lwin.
