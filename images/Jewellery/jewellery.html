Jade

Officially, including jade, ruby and other gems are Burma's(Myanmar's) fifth-largest export. In Myanmar,jade is called "kyauk sein",which literally means "green stone". Jade is geologically classified into two types:nephrite and jadeite.
The latter type that is harder, has more lustre and is more valuable is found in only five countries -Myanmar, Japn, the U.S.,Guatemala and Russia.
Myanmar produces approximately 70 percent of the world's supply for high-quality jadeitd. Most of the Myanmar's jadeite is exported to other nations,primarily Asian, for use in jewelry,art and ornaments. Jadeite from the other countries cannot compare with unique, tone, texture and translucence of that which comes from upper Myanmar.Jade has long been used in religious rituals and as a symbol of power and wealth for emperors and dignitaries

Diamond

With few naturally occurring deposits in Myanmar, diamonds are not as economically productive as othe precious stones such as rubies,jades and sapphires.Myanmar has only small deposits in Mong Mit Township in Shan State, and Myeik township in Tanintharyi Division.
Myanmar diamonds are roughly classified into two types: white (or colourless) and yellowish.White diamonds are more expensive than yellow ones. Flaws on the surface of either type will reduce their value. Diamond sizes in Myanmar range from .01 to 20 rati ,and the stones
are graded into classes based on colour, brightness and clarity.

Maw-sit-sit

This is a green and black gem material that has been confused with jade for hundreds of years. It was once thought to be a variety of jade because of its similar appearance and physical properties, but it's not a jade. It's considered to be a rock rather than a mineral, since it is composed of a number of different
materials, including kosmolar(a mineral related to jadeite) and albite feldspar. Maw-sit-sit (named from Maw-sit-sit village)deposits are in a small area of Kachin State of northern Myanmar.

Amber

Amber is a resin material that is formed from fossilized conifer tree sap during years of constant pressure and heat and contains inclusions, such as insects,whole or parts of animals and plants that are trapped and preserved.One of the oldest amber deposists in the world, dating back 100 million years is located in the Northern region of Myanmar.Myanmar amber is plentiful,quality, and contains inclusions within the resin. These yellow to reddish-brown material has been used in a number of ways,including to make 'Jewelry and in the healing Arts'.

Coral

The middle Ayeyarwady region and apart from it, Rakhine and Tenintharyi Coast are well known for their richness of coral reefs. Myeik Archipelago is composed of more than 800 islands and also well known for its beautiful coral reefs.

Pearl

Myanmar is one of the few countries whose pristine clean waters around the 800 islands of the Myeik Archipelago are the ideal cultivating place for South Sea pearls. Out of the world's best pearls, Myanmar is the only one country which owns the golden pearls. Myanmar's golden pearls come with a unique pinkish, apricot hue, a thicker layer of nacre and large size, and are widely perceived as the best in the world. Both white and golden pearls are produced by local water inhabitants, the Pinctada maxima oyster. The gold lipped oysters produce the golden colored pearls while the silver lipped ones produce shite and silver pearls. Pearls are sold loose in lots or parcels,and also as finished ornaments.
