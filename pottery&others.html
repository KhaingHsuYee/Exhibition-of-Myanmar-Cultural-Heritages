Myanmar's Pottery & Others

Myanmar Pottery

Pottery has a long tradition in Myanmar and is one of the oldest and most widespread of the decorative arts, consisting of objects made of clay and hardened with heat. Pots were not only used to store or to cook food and such as vessels for holding liquids but also to hold gold and jewelry inside them.
The pot industry in Yandabo is the dominant economy. Almost all the villagers in the northern part of town work together to craft the clay pots, which sell for around K1500 in Mandalay. And Sagaing Pottery art is also known as a simple traditional handicraft which is making by the local raw material without using any advanced hi-techniques

Rattan

One of the Myanmar's traditional and creative local products are things made in 'Rattan'.Rattan is an equally important and useful material for the Burmese people. They use a cane in building house, making boats and carts, and in tying packages and also whipping to beat a cattle. Moreover, the craftsmen and women begin the weaving process which transform the materials to products by 100% handmade. You can buy many a wide range of items made in rattan such as serving trays in different shapes,kitchen & office utensils, home decor objects, laundry baskets, storage bins and baskets, chairs,tissue boxes,lamps and etc.Now, the rattan products are widely distributed in the market of USA,Canada, Europe, Japan, South Korea, South Africa, Australia and New Zealand and other countries.

Other Tiny & Adorable Souvenirs

Clay toys

Traditional Toy Pots(Oh-Pote) are still popular until now. In the old days before foreign toys were not much available in the city, children played with traditional toys. The clay toys came in the shape of animals such as bulls,turtles,deer,owls or implements such as pots,mortars, tray sets, and baskets Figurine are also common. For the purpose to love Myanmar traditional, annual festival of Traditional Toy Pot is held in Mandalay.Though they might seem unsophisticated, these colorful clay toys are eco-friendly and affordable.

Paper Mache Toys

Another unique hand-crafted gifts are "Traditional papier-mache toys" like (pyit taing htaung-an egg-shaped face weighted at the bottom),zee kwat(golden owl) and nwar yote(painted oxen). They are made strips of glue-soaked newspaper plastered around a wooden mould and left to bake in the sun.Usually sold at stalls near pagodas entrances and the best and portable souvenirs that you should buy as souvenirs.
