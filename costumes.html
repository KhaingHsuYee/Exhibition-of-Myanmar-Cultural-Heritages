About Myanmar Ethnic Groups

Burma(Myanmar) is a union of 135 ethnic groups with their own languages and dialects.
The major races are the Kachin, the Kayah, the Kayin, the Chin, the Burma, the Mon, the Rahkhine,and the Shan...

Kachin

Kachin men wear shirts, traditional jackets, sarong-like longyi or slacks.They also wear turbans or headdresses with tasseels, han ging loose one end on the right of their head. They keep a sword and a shoulder bag as an ornament. Kachin women adorn themselves with hand-woven clothes with flowery and checkered designs. A number of silver coins and studs are attached to their blouses Kachin ethnic group is made up of a large number of different sub-groups.
There are 12 different tribes such as Kachin, Taron,Jingphaw,Lisu,Lashi,Rawang,Guari,Hkahku,Duleng,Maru,Dalaun and Duleng.

You can see different colorful dresses in fantastic patterns of Kachin traditional and moderndresses.

Kayah

Kayah State is a fairly small region in east of Myanmar.Kayah comprises (9) different ethnic groups . Kayah men wear white headdress and shirts with traditional jackets and trousers just past the knees. Silver daggers and stringed silver swords are carried on traditional occasions.Some Kayah women wear their hair in high knots wrapped with red headresses. Their sleeveless blouses are normally black, covering only one shoulder. Red cloaks are worn over the blouses.Long white long shawls are tied around the waist with both ends hanging loose in front. They usually wear red or black longyis.

Consequently, one of the sub-groups of Karen,Red Karenni's special customs is the most strangest things you'd ever seen. Such as 'long-necked' women that wear brass rings around their necks to give the illusion of having long slender necks.The small group that does this is known as the Kayan Lahwi or Padaung. They begin wear these coils at the age of five and add more rings as the years go on.

Kayin

One of the large number of different sub groups. Typically, Kayin men wear their headdresses with tassels hanging loose on the right side of the head. They wear trousers, longyis, shirts and tunics, which can be either pullover-type or jacket-type. Their longyis have horizontal stripes with a parallel strip in the middle. Kayin women dress in long tunics and longyis, with headbands that have both ends hanging in the front.

Chin

The Chin is also a big ethnic group of different tribes , speaking between 40 and 50 different dialects and so having kind of variety ethnic tribes.Typically,The dresses they wear are extremely colorful-mostly red, green and black. Chin men usually wear shirts and trousers but wrap themselves with colourful blankets on special occasions. They wear headdresses with vertical colourful blankets on special occasions. They wear headdresses with vertical black stripes. Chin women wear longyis long enough to cover their ankles, and decorated with horizontal stripes, diamonds,or flower designs.Their open-front blouses buttoned in the centre, with short sleeves with checkered designs along the edge. They also wear a broad band of silver and bronze wires around their waists. Mon

The Mon, made up of just One group as opposed to the many varying ones that comprise the other Seven races. The earliest major group of Myanmar. Mon men wear red checkered longyis, shirts without collars and traditional jackets and Mon women wrap their long hair around a comb and wear longyis and open-fronted blouses that button in the center.

Bamar

Remain as the majority ethnicity in the coutry. Bamar are a Sino-Tibetan ethnic group that takes up roughly two-thirds of the population of Myanmar, typically burmese culture are derived from this ethnicity .

In dressing,Bamar men dress in longyis, shirts without collars and traditional jackets. They also wear ready-made turbans with a wing-cloth standing to the right. Bamar women wear longyis and blouuses with an opening in the front, which are buttoned either in the centre or on the side. They wear their hair in top-knots and drape lace shawls. And also,"Velvet Sandals" are worn on theit feet. Moreover, Bamar women often put a yellowy white powder called'thanaka'on their faces.

You can see different colorful dresses in fantastic patterns of Burmese traditional and moderndresses.

Rakhine

Rakhine ,also known as "Arakanese" which is closely related to the Bamar people. Rakhine men wears delicately woven longyis, shirts without collars and traditional jackets. They also wear ready-made turbans with the wing-cloth standing to the left. Rahkhine women wear their hair in a variety of styles. They wear front-opening blouses buttoned either in the centre or on the side. Their longyis are woven in beautiful designs usuallly consisting of horizontal stripes. A shawl is wrapped across the body passing over the left shoulder.

These are beautiful Rakhine traditional costumes.

Shan

The second largest group is Shan after Bamar.Shan men dress in shirts and traditinal khaki jackets. Their baggy trousers are usually made of khaki. Every Shan man wears a headdress. Shan women wear with longuis, blouses, and headdresses. Moreover,Shan's ethnicity dressing is interestinng. The married men and women wear turbans,and the color is an important part of Shan commuties Young girls start wearing brightly colored dresses at the age of 14,and as they get older, the outfits gradually become less colourful. The men on the other hand stick to dark blue baggy trousers. The women in age 40 starts wearing big black clothes as representing for the rest of their lives.

You can see different colorful dresses in fantastic patterns of Shan.
